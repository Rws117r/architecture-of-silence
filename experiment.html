PROTOCOLS

This section documents the four primary avenues of structural intervention. These protocols seek to disrupt the recursive loop of failure and explore materials of flexibility, recalibration, and controlled relief.

PROTOCOL ID: EXP-PR-01
PENDING // ARCHIVAL PERMIT

Hypervigilance
Loop Intervention

When the structural mask cracks, the system misinterprets failure as "insufficient control," triggering perpetual hypervigilance. This accelerates the recursive loop, ensuring future ruptures happen with increasing velocity.

NEXT STEP // OBJECTIVE

Disrupt the post-rupture learning phase. Design a mechanism to force the system to diagnose 'over-control' rather than 'insufficient control' following a fracture event.

PROTOCOL ID: EXP-ME-02
SIMULATION // STAGE 1

Psychological
Pressure Valve

The current model suffers from "absolute retention." The internal ribs can only add tension, lacking any mechanism to simplify or de-escalate. The system assumes the mask has infinite capacity until it exceeds its threshold.

NEXT STEP // OBJECTIVE

Introduce a controlled discharge mechanism. Design a way for the system to safely vent internal pressure from the 'Lantern' and 'Oculus' before structural failure occurs.

PROTOCOL ID: EXP-IT-03
RE-CALIBRATION // IN PROGRESS

Oculus Metadata
Recalibration

The Oculus operates on "Prevention over Accuracy," interpreting neutral events—like silence—as immediate risks. These false alarms force the system into unnecessary, destructive over-engineering.

NEXT STEP // OBJECTIVE

Tamper with the interpretive layer. Force the Oculus to prioritize accuracy over risk-detection to stop the 'Mechanical Clinch' at the point of translation.

PROTOCOL ID: EXP-BI-04
DEFERRED // ETHICAL REVIEW

Boundary Interface
Flexibility

The concrete mask is rigid with zero adaptability. Sustained microfractures are accumulative and irreversible. The more effective the mask is at containment, the more catastrophic the eventual rupture becomes.

NEXT STEP // OBJECTIVE

Explore architectural metaphors for elasticity. Contrast the concrete barrier with materials designed to bend, breathe, or self-repair under high atmospheric stress.
